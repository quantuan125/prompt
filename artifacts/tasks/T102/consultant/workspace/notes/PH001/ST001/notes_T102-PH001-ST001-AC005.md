10) Program advisory: Short migration notice in `prompt/AGENTS.md`.

#### B. Narrative Summary (Minutes-Style)

This plan amendment session arose from AC005's successful golden exemplar completion, which revealed a fundamental architectural inversion: normative specifications (CLAUSEs) live inside ADRs (decision records), while STDs (standards) merely "adopt" ADRs by reference. This creates two synchronized surfaces and a semantic mismatch — the specification authority should derive from the standard, not the decision record.

The consultant initially assessed the ADR→STD migration as architecturally sound but premature for Phase 1, recommending Option A (defer to Phase 2) to preserve the Phase 1 stability contract. The assessment identified the blast radius as massive: every `T102-ADR-004-CLAUSE-nnn` reference across SPS, Concept, guidelines, templates, notes, and plans would become stale. The consultant identified three options: Option A (recommended — document Phase 2 target, complete Phase 1 as-is), Option B (partial — add CLAUSE-018 documenting the migration aspiration), and Option C (aggressive — execute the migration now, accept the rework).

The client overrode the recommendation with a clear directive: **Option C is the path**. The rationale: since all ADRs outside Concept must eventually be migrated anyway, it is better to invest in getting T102-STD-004 correct as the absolute golden exemplar before parallel executions. Phase 1 decisions are informative (not normative) unless explicitly documented in the SSOT file, so the stability contract does not block a governed rename. All other streams (ST002, ST003) are blocked until AC006 completes.

The client additionally asked whether to keep the `CLAUSE` token or introduce a new `SPEC` token. The consultant's analysis, grounded in ISO/IEC governance models, confirmed that "clause" is the correct industry term for normative subdivisions of a standard. In ISO, specifications (clauses) live inside standards — they don't exist independently. A clause without a parent standard has no authority. Introducing `SPEC` would be inventing non-standard terminology. The recommendation: keep `CLAUSE`, reparent it under `STD` by creating a new `STDCID` (Standard Clause ID) category in the ADR-005 taxonomy, while marking the existing `DRCID` (Decision Record Clause ID) as legacy.

The combined-file structure under the new model reverses section order: `## Specification` (normative CLAUSEs) comes first, `## Decision Record` (informative rationale annex) comes second — aligning with ISO convention where normative content precedes informative annexes. The `## Decision` heading is renamed to `## Decision Record` for semantic clarity.

Three additional design decisions were resolved through Q&A: (1) Concept gets a separate "Initiative STD Index" alongside the existing ADR Index, with T102-STD-004 migrated there while remaining ADRs stay in the ADR Index until their ST003 migration; (2) CLAUSE-016 is rewritten in-place for the STD model rather than deprecated; (3) ST003 migration scope includes all T102 scope levels (initiative + epic), not just initiative-level.

AC006 is added to ST001 as a critical activity with 12 tasks spanning taxonomy updates, golden exemplar rebuild, derivative alignment, migration scripting, and self-consistency validation. The critical path is TK001→TK002→TK003→TK004→TK005→TK011→TK012 with parallel opportunities after TK005 (SPS, guideline/template, migration script) and after TK004 (program advisory).
