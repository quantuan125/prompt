#### B. Narrative Summary (Minutes-Style)

This consultation continues AC006 (STD-Contains-CLAUSE governance migration) following the 2026-02-06 plan-amendment session (DEC004–DEC015). The focus of this session is to remove execution ambiguity by (a) resolving STD/ADR structural decisions and (b) expanding AC006 tasks TK003–TK012 with concrete deliverables and constraints suitable for LLM_Developer implementation.

Key outcomes:
- **STD-level structure** is formalized: `## References` and `## Provenance` are promoted to STD-level `##` sections, rather than being embedded inside the Decision Record body.
- `CLAUSE-016` is rewritten to define the **canonical combined-file structure** for STD files that contain both normative specification CLAUSEs and an informative nested ADR.
- **ADR nesting** is confirmed as **STD-internal**: ADR IDs are constructed as `<STD-ID>-ADR-###` (Model B), and only one current ADR exists per STD file at a time (update-in-place model with history tracked via changelog/git).
- **Migration script** requirements are made explicit: Python implementation, excludes `raw_*` files, performs same-number ADR→STD transformations, and MUST NOT reorder sections (manual one-time operation for the golden exemplar).
- **Plan governance** is strengthened by defining a formal Gate construct and inserting **`T102-PH001-ST001-AC006-GATE-001`** after TK005 to pause derivative work for client review/approval.

Additional confirmations:
- `STDCID` is a peer category to legacy `DRCID` (no `GID` category is introduced).
- The **Context** field is narrative and may reference IDs in shorthand; the formal list of references belongs in the STD-level `## References` section and must not contain “Governed By” IDs.
- The `## Decision Record` heading is used without ADR numbering; the ADR ID appears as the first line inside the section using the DR body pattern.
