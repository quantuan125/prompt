# RESEARCH BRIEF: Comprehensive Analysis of Concept Artifact & Dynamic Workspace Model

## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This research brief commissions a comprehensive analysis of the current `concept_T102` artifact and its role as a "dynamic workspace" within the consultancy layer architecture. The research aims to validate our approach against industry standards, identify structural and procedural optimization opportunities, and provide actionable recommendations for developing the T102C-CST and T102C-CPG features.

## II. RESEARCH SCOPE & OBJECTIVES

### **Primary Research Questions**

1. **Industry Mapping & Classification**
   - What industry frameworks, standards, or methodologies most closely align with our "dynamic workspace" Concept artifact?
   - Is our Concept artifact functionally equivalent to an Architectural Description Document (ADD) per ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010, or does it represent a hybrid/novel approach?
   - How do similar artifacts function in established methodologies (SAFe, Scaled Agile, TOGAF, Zachman, etc.)?

2. **Workflow Integration & Procedural Optimization**
   - What are the established best practices for managing dynamic, cross-scope decision aggregation in the "Develop & Deliver" phase of the Double Diamond model?
   - How do industry leaders handle the tension between stable documents (governance/requirements) and dynamic workspaces (architectural decisions)?
   - What procedural frameworks exist for maintaining coherence across fragmented artifacts while preventing decision drift?

3. **Structural Analysis & Gap Assessment**
   - Comparative analysis of our current `concept_T102` structure against industry-standard ADDs, SADs, and similar artifacts
   - Identification of structural strengths, weaknesses, and residual gaps in our current approach
   - Assessment of integration effectiveness with SPS (PID-like) and Request (SRS+BRD-like) artifacts

4. **Risk Mitigation & Issue Resolution**
   - Industry approaches to addressing the specific challenges we've identified:
     - Document bloat and responsibility overload (T102C-RISK-01)
     - ADR/GDR separation and drift prevention (T102C-RISK-02)
     - LLM agent context management across stable/dynamic documents (T102C-RISK-03)
     - Handoff locus determination (T102-ISSUE-04)
     - ADR canonicalization location (T102-ISSUE-05)
     - Readiness tracking mechanisms (T102-ISSUE-07)

### **Secondary Research Objectives**

5. **Enhancement Opportunities**
   - Structural improvements to the Concept artifact framework
   - Procedural optimizations for the dynamic workspace approach
   - Integration pattern recommendations for seamless handoffs to LLM_Planner

6. **Feature Development Guidance**
   - Recommendations for T102C-CST (Concept Structural Template) development priorities
   - Best practices for T102C-CPG (Concept Procedural Guideline) authoring workflows
   - Template design patterns that maximize reusability across initiatives
