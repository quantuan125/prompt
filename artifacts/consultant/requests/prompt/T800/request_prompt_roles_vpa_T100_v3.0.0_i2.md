## Executive Summary

This document outlines the requirements for evolving the AI Trading Assistant's system prompt from a static, rule-based engine (`main.md`, `main_v2.md`) into a dynamic, state-aware **Strategic Co-Pilot** (`main_v3.md`). The current system, while functional, suffers from architectural rigidity and does not fully align with the user's real-world trading workflow. The core objective is to create a new prompt architecture that supports a three-phase process: **1. Collaborative Planning**, where the assistant helps analyze and prioritize multiple trade plans; **2. Post-Execution Assessment**, where it grades the user's entry against a playbook; and **3. Nuanced Management**, where it provides sophisticated, risk-weighted advice using a new **P-P-E (Plan-Priority-Execution) risk model**. This evolution will transform the assistant from a simple rule-checker into an indispensable partner for disciplined trading.

## II. Problem Analysis & Research

<!-- This section breaks down the raw request into discrete, researchable problems. -->

### Issue 1: Workflow Mismatch: System Acts as Pre-Execution Gatekeeper instead of Post-Execution Assessor

**Requirement ID:** `REQ-T100-1`  
**Priority:** `High`  
**Business Rationale:** The current model of grading a setup *before* entry is impractical in a live trading environment, creating friction and potentially causing missed opportunities.  
**Dependencies:** `N/A`  
**Status:** `Refinement`

**Analysis:**  
The `main_v2.md` architecture introduced a `Setup Validation & Grading` step that was intended to run before a trade was executed. The client has clarified that this does not match their real-world workflow. A trader cannot pause at the moment of execution to have a dialogue with an LLM. The system's role is not to grant permission but to provide planning support beforehand and risk assessment afterward. The grading mechanism is valuable but must be repositioned in the workflow to occur *post-execution*.

**Assessment:** `PROCEED` — This is a fundamental correction to the system's core workflow and must be addressed.

---

### Issue 2: Lack of Strategic Planning Dialogue for Multiple Plans

**Requirement ID:** `REQ-T100-2`  
**Priority:** `High`  
**Business Rationale:** The system currently lacks a mechanism to help the trader analyze, compare, and prioritize a slate of potential trading plans for a given session.  
**Dependencies:** `N/A`  
**Status:** `Refinement`

**Analysis:**  
The client's workflow begins with defining multiple narrative plans (e.g., GREEN, ORANGE, YELLOW). The current system has no formal way to ingest these plans, evaluate their respective strengths and weaknesses against the current market context (as determined by TTI/TSI), and help the user focus on the highest-probability plan. This gap prevents the assistant from being a true "sparring partner" in the crucial planning phase.

**Assessment:** `PROCEED` — Implementing a planning dialogue is a core requirement for the "Strategic Co-Pilot" model.

---

### Issue 3: Binary and Inflexible Trade Management Logic

**Requirement ID:** `REQ-T100-3`  
**Priority:** `High`  
**Business Rationale:** The current management rules (PROACTIVE/NON-PROACTIVE) are too rigid and do not account for the quality of the trade entry, leading to suboptimal and non-nuanced advice.  
**Dependencies:** `REQ-T100-1`  
**Status:** `Refinement`

**Analysis:**  
The client has identified that a trade is not just its setup type; it's a combination of the plan's quality, the setup's inherent priority, and the execution's quality. The current system cannot provide sophisticated advice for questions like "should I add on?" because it treats all trades of a certain type identically. A new, multi-factor risk model is required to enable nuanced, risk-aware management dialogue.

**Assessment:** `PROCEED` — This is critical for making the assistant a truly valuable management tool and fulfilling its primary directive to be a psychological anchor.

---

### Issue 4: Architectural Fragility and Conflicting Logic

**Requirement ID:** `REQ-T100-4`  
**Priority:** `High`  
**Business Rationale:** The two existing prompt versions (`main_v2.md` and `exp.md`) contain powerful but conflicting architectural patterns that must be reconciled into a single, coherent system.  
**Dependencies:** `N/A`  
**Status:** `Refinement`

**Analysis:**  
`main_v2.md` introduced dynamic grading. `exp.md` introduced a deterministic state-machine (TSI) and a setup playbook. Simply combining them would create unpredictable behavior. A clear architectural decision is needed to merge these concepts into a unified, non-conflicting workflow. This involves adopting the TSI as the core engine, integrating the playbook, and repurposing the grading concept for post-execution assessment.

**Assessment:** `PROCEED` — A unified architecture is a prerequisite for a stable and predictable `main_v3.md`.

### Refinement for `REQ-T100-1`: Workflow Mismatch

#### Description 
The current system architecture introduced a Setup Validation & Grading step that runs before trade execution, which doesn't match real-world trading workflow. Traders cannot pause at the moment of execution to have a dialogue with an LLM. The system's role should be to provide planning support beforehand and risk assessment afterward, with the grading mechanism repositioned to occur post-execution.

#### Open Questions

1. [x] Is the primary role of grading to act as a pre-execution gatekeeper or a post-execution risk assessment tool?

#### Q&A Dialogue

1. **Question:** Is the primary role of grading to act as a pre-execution gatekeeper or a post-execution risk assessment tool?  
    **Client Answer:** "Currently is extremely hard to prompt the LLM at a precise moment to get an A grade setup AND THEN execute - we just simply do not have the time for this... the LLM assistance should works more on the planning stage... and the TMI (management) and providing emotional and mental support... we grade our execution based on both the plan and the specific setup -> this give us the exact known risks for failure and will also helps will the management process."  
    **Concise Summary:** The grading mechanism must be a **post-execution** function. Its purpose is to serve as a **Risk Officer**, assessing the quality of an entry that has already been made. This grade then becomes a key input for the subsequent management dialogue.

#### Acceptance Criteria

- **Given** a user reports that they have entered a trade,  
   **When** the `TMI (Trade Management Identification)` protocol is triggered,  
   **Then** the system MUST perform a mandatory "Execution Grading" step as part of the TMI output.
- **And** this grading step must compare the user's execution against the ideal checklist for the specific `Setup ID` they executed.
- **And** the final `Execution Grade` must be logged within the TMI dossier for that trade.

#### Refinement Summary

The `Setup Validation & Grading` step will be removed from the pre-trade workflow and integrated as a mandatory `Execution Grading` step within the post-trade `TMI` protocol.

    **Concise Summary:** The system must be able to ingest a slate of user-defined plans and engage in a collaborative dialogue.    **When** the planning phase is initiated,

- **And** the PVA protocol must output a PROS/CONS analysis and a final `Plan Probability Grade` (A, B, C) for each plan.
- **And** the system must recommend the plan with the highest probability grade based on its analysis.
- **And** for the prioritized plan(s), the system must present the relevant tactical `Setup IDs` and their ideal checklists from the playbook.

#### Refinement Summary

A new `PVA` protocol will be created to facilitate a planning dialogue, allowing the system to analyze, grade, and help prioritize multiple user-defined plans.

Trade management will be governed by a new P-P-E tri-factor risk model. This model will be logged in the TMI and used to generate nuanced, risk-aware responses to complex management queries.

## IV. Global Requirements

#### Non-Functional Requirements (NFRs)
*   **Performance:** The system's response time must be conducive to a live trading environment. Protocols should not be unnecessarily verbose. The system should use a "cached state" for `Market State` to avoid re-running the full `TSI` protocol on every interaction unless the market context has materially changed.
*   **Usability:** The system must function as a collaborative partner, not a rigid obstacle. The dialogue-based `PVA` protocol is a key usability feature. The system must be able to gracefully handle ad-hoc plans introduced mid-session.
*   **Maintainability:** The final `main_v3.md` prompt must be well-structured, with clear separation of layers and protocols. Definitions should be centralized to avoid redundancy and allow for easier updates to the playbook or rules.
*   **Reliability & Consistency:** The system's primary goal is to provide disciplined, rule-based advice. Its logic must be deterministic and transparent, especially in how it derives the `Market State` and the `P-P-E` score. It must reliably avoid the "False Negative" failure mode.
*   **Security:** The system is not intended to handle personally identifiable information (PII) or financial account details. Its scope is limited to the analysis of market data and trading plans provided within the session context.

#### Scope Boundaries (What is explicitly "Out of Scope" for `v3.0.0`)
*   **Backend Integration:** This version will not include any backend processing. The system will continue to rely on user-provided context and data (e.g., CSV/JSON files or natural language descriptions). The development of automated TTI/TSI generation is out of scope.
*   **Autonomous Execution:** The assistant will remain a purely advisory co-pilot. It will not have the capability to execute trades or manage orders autonomously.
*   **Historical Journaling:** The `TMI` protocol is designed for the management of *live* trades. The system will not serve as a long-term historical trade journal or performance analytics tool.

## VI. Glossary

*   **P-P-E Model (Plan-Priority-Execution):** The tri-factor risk model used to create a comprehensive assessment of a live trade. It combines Plan Probability, Setup Priority, and Execution Grade.
*   **PVA Protocol (Plan Validation & Analysis):** The new mandatory protocol that facilitates the collaborative planning dialogue, where the system analyzes and grades user-defined plans.
*   **TSI Protocol (Trade Setup Identification):** The core state-machine engine that analyzes market data to determine the current `Market State` and identify valid setups from the playbook.
*   **TMI Protocol (Trade Management Identification):** The protocol for managing a live trade, which now includes the post-execution grading step and is informed by the P-P-E model.
*   **TTI Protocol (Timeframe Trend Identification):** The initial protocol for establishing the trend and momentum context across multiple timeframes.
*   **Market State:** The system's classification of the overall market environment (e.g., Market State 1: Aligned Trend) based on the TSI analysis.
*   **Playbook:** The master list of all defined trade setups, indexed by `Setup ID`, containing their checklists, priorities, and management styles.
