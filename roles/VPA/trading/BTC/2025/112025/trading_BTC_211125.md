## MACRO CONTEXT
### LAST WEEK

* **Fed: split messaging but tilt toward more cuts.**

  * Atlanta Fed President **Bostic** (Nov 12) flagged inflation as the “clear and urgent” risk and warned that easing too fast could “feed the inflation beast,” arguing for caution on further cuts.
  * Governor **Waller** (Nov 17) laid out “The Case for Continuing Rate Cuts,” arguing that a softening labor market and slowing inflation justify more easing.
  * Today, **NY Fed President Williams** said policy is “modestly restrictive” and there is “room for a further adjustment in the near term” toward neutral, reinforcing that a **December cut is still on the table**, even as some Fed officials push back.
  * Net effect: markets see a **live, but not guaranteed, December cut**, with front-end yields and risk sentiment whipsawing intraday around Fed headlines.

* **Trump, tariffs & the Supreme Court.**

  * On **Nov 5**, the Supreme Court heard arguments on Trump’s use of the **1977 IEEPA emergency law** to impose sweeping global tariffs; justices across the spectrum were notably skeptical, but the tariffs remain in force while the Court deliberates, with a ruling expected in early 2026.
  * On **Nov 9**, Trump announced a plan to send most Americans **“tariff dividend” checks of at least $2,000**, funded from tariff revenues, prompting warnings from tax and budget experts that revenues may not cover the cost.
  * On **Nov 10–11**, he warned that a Court defeat would mean **“economic disaster”** and a national-security threat, doubling down on tariffs as the centerpiece of his economic strategy.
  * Analysts note that even if the Court strikes down the IEEPA tariffs, the administration could rebuild a **similarly aggressive tariff regime using other trade laws**, so trade-policy uncertainty and tariff-linked inflation pressure remain a medium-term overhang for growth, the Fed, and risk assets.

* **Trade structure & exemptions.**

  * The broad **100% tariffs on most Chinese and many other imports that took effect on Nov 1** remain in place, but the administration is already tweaking them at the margin: exemptions for more than 200 food products (including some dairy and specialty items) kicked in mid-November and a bilateral deal eased tariffs on certain Swiss cheese exports.
  * Combined with a fragile truce with China that paused some retaliatory tariffs, this has **not reduced overall tariff intensity**, but it has shifted burdens across sectors and kept supply-chain and inflation risks in focus.

* **Geopolitics: Trump’s Ukraine peace plan jolts allies.**

  * A **28-point U.S. peace proposal** for the Russia–Ukraine war leaked this week, reportedly requiring Ukraine to cede occupied territory, cap its military and renounce NATO membership.
  * Ukrainian officials and European leaders reacted sharply, describing elements of the plan as a **near “capitulation”** and insisting Europe and Kyiv must be central to any talks. Leaders from Germany, France, the UK and Ukraine rushed into **emergency consultations** to coordinate a response.
  * The proposal adds a new layer of **geopolitical and NATO-credibility risk**, supportive of safe-haven demand in **gold and the dollar**, and potentially negative for European assets and high-beta risk (including crypto) if the controversy deepens.

* **Global markets: AI bubble fears, worst week since spring.**

  * Despite **blowout earnings and guidance from Nvidia**, equities reversed sharply: several sessions saw strong tech-led rallies fade into deep red closes as investors questioned whether **AI valuations have overshot**.
  * The **S&P 500** and Nasdaq registered multiple 0.8–1.5% down days, the semiconductor index fell into **correction**, and the **VIX spiked to its highest close since April**, leaving major U.S. indices on track for their **worst weekly drop since at least April**. Even after the sell-off, the S&P 500 remains up low-teens percent year-to-date and the Nasdaq high-teens, so positioning was extended.
  * The risk-off spilled into crypto: **Bitcoin and Ether dropped more than 10% in 24 hours at one point**, with thin order-book liquidity amplifying moves and several exchanges seeing flash crashes toward ~80k in BTC.
  * **U.S. spot BTC ETFs have recorded record net outflows (~$3.8B) in November**, with **ETH ETFs losing ~$1.8B**, while newer **SOL and XRP ETFs enjoy net inflows**, signaling forced de-risking and rotation away from the top two coins.
  * **Gold**, after breaking above **$4,000/oz to new all-time highs in October** and logging ~50–60% YTD gains, has been **chopping just above the $4,000 handle**. It spiked earlier this month on expectations of a December Fed cut and the end of the U.S. government shutdown, then pulled back but remains near record territory as tariffs, Fed uncertainty and geopolitical risk keep safe-haven demand elevated.

* **Data/shutdown backdrop.**

  * The **U.S. government shutdown ended last week**, but it delayed the release of several key data sets (including some October inflation and labor data).
  * As a result, Fed officials are **leaning more on private and survey data** for now, and markets know that **a backlog of “hard” data is about to hit** in the coming week, increasing event risk around rates, USD, gold and equity/crypto beta.
