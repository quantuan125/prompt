### LAST WEEK

* **Trump, shutdown & data blackout end (Wed 12 Nov):** The **43-day U.S. government shutdown – the longest on record – ended when President Trump signed a funding bill on Nov 12**, reopening agencies and restoring pay for ~900k furloughed workers. * **Trump–China truce, fentanyl tariff cut & rare-earths deal (still the backdrop):** Markets continued to digest the **Oct 30 Trump–Xi Busan summit**, where the U.S. agreed to **cut fentanyl-linked tariffs on Chinese goods from 20% to 10% and reduce average China tariffs from ~57% to ~47%**, in exchange for Beijing curbing fentanyl precursors, resuming large U.S. soybean purchases and pausing new rare-earth export controls. This is seen as a **partial de-escalation of U.S.–China trade tensions**, supportive for global growth and risk assets into November.

At the same time, **Switzerland accepted a 15% tariff deal with the Trump administration**, ending a dispute over Swiss exports and signaling that Trump 2.0 is still using tariffs tactically even with allies.
